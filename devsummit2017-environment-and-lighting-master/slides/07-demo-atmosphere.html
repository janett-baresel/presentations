view.environment.atmosphereEnabled = true;
view.environment.atmosphere.quality = "low";

// set atmosphere quality to "high"
view.environment.atmosphereEnabled = true;
view.environment.atmosphere.quality = "high";

- there's basically 3 different atmosphere settings
- completely disabled, low quality (usually the default) and high quality
- the atmosphere influences the appearance of the sky, the horizon, as well as - well - the atmosphere between the 3d scene and the camera
- in high quality mode we simulate light scattering by dust particles in the air, leading to a useful depth cue - this is particularly useful for this simple base map, for other basemaps it's easier to get by without it - lastly, the atmosphere setting also influences the appeareance at a more global scale - the atmosphere also depends on the current lighting, as can be seen if we start the animation
